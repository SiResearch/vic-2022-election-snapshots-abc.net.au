Profile

On the northern fringes of Melbourne, the main population centre of Yan Yean is Mernda and Doreen, but the electorate runs south to include Plenty, and north to take in Donnybrook, Whittlesea, Heathcote Junction, Wandong and parts of Wallan. Yan Yean covers 611 square kilometres.

Redistribution

Previously well over quota, some major excisions were made to Yan Yean's boundaries. Around 10,000 voters near Diamond Creek were transferred to Eltham, another 10,000 in Wallan and Beveridge transferred to the new seat of Kalkallo, and around 3,000 voters in Hurstbrige are now in Eidon. Despite the changes, Labor's margin declines only a percentage point from 17.0% to an estimated 16.9%.

Background

Yan Yean was first contested at the 1992 election and has been won by Labor at every election since. The seat's first MP was Andre Haermeyer, elected in 1992, but he moved to the new seat of Kororoit in 2002 when the 2000/2001 redistribution produced boundaries that turned Yan Yean into a notional Liberal seat.

Haermeyer was succeeded in Yan Yean by Labor's current MP Danielle Green, who easily overcame the boundary changes in 2002 to retain Yan Yean with a swing of more than 10% in her favour. She easily retained the seat in both 2006 and 2010.

Another redistribution required Green to gain a swing in her favour to win re-election in 2014, a task easily achieved. Green added another 13.4% swing to her margin in 2018. Green is retiring at the 2022 election.

Past Results

The graph below plots the 2-party preferred results for Whittlesea 1985-88, and Yan Yean with an estimate for 1988, gains the 2-party preferred vote for Victoria as a whole. As the graph shows, Yan Yean has traditionally been stronger for Labor than Victoria as a whole. Yan Yean's position perched on Melbourne's northern fringe means that the seat's political complexion can be radically altered by redistributions. Redistributions ahead of both the 2002 and 2014 elections gave Yan Yean a notional Liberal margin, but Labor recovered the seat easily at both elections. Labor's substantial margin after the 2018 election meant the most recent redistribution did not imperil Labor's hold on the seat.

Richard Welch

Liberal

Welch founded his own business after patenting a new form of motion-tracking technology, and spent 12 years growing his business by travelling through the UK, India, the UAE and South Africa. After an extensive career as CEO of his own company, Welch and his wife returned to Australia so their children could grow up close to their Australian relatives. Wlech's experience in business includes supply chain management, manufacturing and import/export industries. Welch has a Master of Business Administration from Imperial College, London, and believes in the power of education to help people achieve their goals. He was the Liberal candidate for the local seat of McEwen at the Federal election in May.

Website

Lauren Kathage

Australian Labor Party - Victorian Branch

Kathage was born in South Australia and grew up in Queensland, eventually working in the Northern Territory before moving to Victoria more than a decade ago with her husband. Kathage spent more than 15 years as a community worker in Australia and internationally, leading small housing and homelessness projects. She has also managed multimillion-dollar programs to train healthcare workers, improve income generation opportunities for regional communities and boost the school attendance rates of children with disabilities.
