MP

Sonya Kilkenny (Labor) since the 2014 election. Minister for Corrections, Minister for Youth Justice, for Victim Support, and Minister for Fishing and Boating.

Profile

Carrum is a south-east Melbourne bayside seat just north of Frankston, including Bonbeach, Carrum, Lyndhurst Patterson Lakes and parts Seaford before extending east away Port Phillip Bay to include Carrum Downs, Sandhurst, Lyndhurst and Skye. The electorate covers 86.6 square kilometres.

Redistribution

Gains around 4,500 voters in Lyndhurst from Cranbourne while losing 4,200 voters in Seaford to Frankston. The Labor margin nudges upward from 11.9% to an estimated 12.0%.

Background

Carrum has been held by the Labor Party for all but two terms since it was contested in 1976. It is a bellwether electorate, having been won by the party forming government at all seven elections since 1996, and nine of the ten elections since 1985. Carrum is one of the four Frankston rail line seats that put the Baillieu government into office in 2010, and returned Labor to power in 2014.

Carrum was represented by Labor's Mal Sandon from 1988 until 1996, having previously represented the area in the Legislative Council as a member for Chelsea Province. A minister in the Kirner government, Sandon narrowly survived Labor's 1992 wipeout. His continued presence on the Labor frontbench in opposition allowed the Liberal Party to reprise its 1992 'Guilty Party' campaign for the 1996 election when the Liberal Party's David Lean defeating Sandon. Carrum was the only seat gained by the Kennett government on its 1996 re-election.

That Liberal victory was reversed in 1999 when Labor's Jenny Lindell won the seat for Labor. Carrum was one of only three Melbourne seats gained by Labor in 1999, the election of the Bracks government owing more to the big swing against the Kennett government in regional Victoria.

Having crept into Labor's column in 1999, Carrum was easily retained by Labor in 2002 and 2006 before Jenny Lindell was defeated by Liberal Donna Bauer in 2010 after an above average swing of 8.7%.

The redistribution before the 2014 election cut Bauer's margin from 2.0% to just 0.3%. While Bauer kept the swing to Labor down to just 1%, it was not enough to prevent Labor's Sonya Kilkenny from winning.

Bauer returned as the Liberal candidate in 2018 when it looked like the Frankston line seats would again be critical. That proved not to be when double digits swings all along the line delivered Labor easy victories, including an 11.2% swing in Carrum.

Polling Place Results 2018

Labor majorities were recorded in 13 of the 14 polling places used at the 2018 election. The Liberal Party recorded 50.3% of the two-party preferred vote at Patterson Lakes Community Centre, but Labor's vote passed 60% in 12 polling places, 70% in four, and reached 74.9% at Rowellyn Park Primary School in Carrum Downs.

Past Results

As the graph below shows, changing boundaries and demography appear to have made Carrum less Labor leaning than it used to be. The gap between the Labor 2-party preferred vote in Carrum and Victoria has been narrow at close elections, but seems to blow out when Labor has a big win. The 2010 election was the first where the Liberal 2-party vote was higher in Carrum than for Victoria. The new boundaries introduced at the 2014 election shifted Carrum slightly Labor's way, but the Liberal Party's rear-guard action depressed the swing back to Labor in 2014. With a new Labor MP in place at the 2018 election, the gap between Carrum and Labor's result for Victoria as a whole again widened.

Bec Buchanan

Liberal

Buchanan and her husband have lived in the Frankston and Carrum electorates for over 48 years. They have four children. Buchanan has spent her professional life working in the Carrum community, having worked with the National Australia Bank (NAB) at the Chelsea branch and worked at Flinders College for 15 years, where her children attended. After undertaking training as a youth worker, Buchanan was a volunteer for four years at Frankston High School and the Frankston YMCA at Delacombe Park.

Website

Sonya Kilkenny

Australian Labor Party - Victorian Branch

Kilkenny was born in Sydney and attended high schools in the United States and Hong Kong before returning to Australia to study Law. Before winning Carrum at the 2014 election Kilkenny worked as a commercial lawyer and also engaged in pro bono work with not-for-profit organisations. Kilkenny has also worked for the Kimberley Land Council, the Australian Children's Television Foundation, and tutored at Monash and Melbourne Universities. She had previously been the Labor candidate for Dunkley at the 2013 Federal election. Kilkenny was first elected in 2014, appointed a Parliamentary Secretary after the 2018 election, became Cabinet Secretary in December 2020 and joined the ministry in July 2022.
