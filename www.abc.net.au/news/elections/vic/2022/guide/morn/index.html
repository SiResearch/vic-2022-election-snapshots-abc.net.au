MP

The retiring Liberal MP is David Morris who has represented the seat since 2006.

Profile

Covers a string of Mornington Peninsula suburbs south of Frankston, including Mount Eliza, Mornington, Mount Martha and Moorooduc. The electorate covers 154.6 square kilometres.

Redistribution

Unchanged.

Background

Mornington was first contested at the 1859 election and existed for more than a century before being abolished in 1967. It was re-created in 1985 and has been Liberal held since.

Mornington was represented by Liberal Robin Cooper from 1985 to 2006, serving as Minister for Transport 1996-99 in the second term of the Kennett government. Cooper was succeeded by David Morris in 2006. Morris is retiring at the 2022 election.

2018 Polling Place Results

Of the 13 polling places used in 2018, nine recorded Liberal majorities against four for Labor. Liberal two-party votes ranged from 37.0% at Mornington Park Primary School to 68.6% at Toorak College in Mt Eliza.

More Information

Kate Lardner

Independent

Lardner has spent her entire life on the Mornington Peninsula. She was born there, attended school in Mt Eliza, became a practising doctor at a local hospital and, recently, began to raise a family there. She is a doctor at Frankston Hospital. With her husband, Lardner founded Healthy Futures, a non-profit organisation that mobilises doctors to address climate change. Lardener is a former member of the Greens and is one of the 'teal' independents supported by the Climate 200 organisation.

Website

Leonie Schween

Animal Justice Party

After an earlier career in secondary teaching, Schween is now a practising lawyer. She has experience in varied areas, including legislation and policy, law reform, planning, environment, local government, privacy, health, aged care/retirement living and financial services. For the past 6 years she has also worked as a volunteer at The Briars historical estate in Mt Martha, caring for the much loved pigs and chickens.

Website

Chris Crewther

Liberal

39 year-old Crewther lives in the local area and ran his own small business in agricultural exports before his election to the House of Representatives as member for Dunkley in 2016. He had previously been the Liberal candidate in 2013 for the north-west Victorian seat of Mallee. Crewther has ANU Masters' degrees in International Law and Diplomacy. Crewther has worked as CEO of the Mildura Development Corporation and as an international lawyer through the United Nations in Kosovo. He is also a Non-Executive Director of Zoe Support Australia, helping young pregnant and parenting mothers get back into education. Crewther is patron of the Tourette Syndrome Association of Australia and is a member of Australia's Modern Slavery Expert Advisory Group.
