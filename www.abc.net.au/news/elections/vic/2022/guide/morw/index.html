MP

The retiring Independent MP is Russell Northe. He was first elected as a National MP in 2006, became an Independent in August 2017 and was re-elected as an Independent in 2018.

Profile

While based in the middle of rural Gippsland, the electorate of Morwell has a very different economic base, still dominated by the coal mining and electricity generation industries despite the closure of some plants. The main towns are spread along the Princes Highway from Moe through Morwell to Traralgon. Morwell is currently held by Independent and ex-National MP Russell Northe, but he is retiring at the 2022 election. With Northe not in the contest, Morwell has a notional Labor majority.

Redistribution

The Draft Redistribution proposed no changes to the boundaries of Morwell, but changes were made at the review stage resulting in Moe neing transferred from Narracan to Morwell. This unites in one electorate the major Latrobe City Council communities along the Princes Highway from Moe through Morwell to Traralgon. It also required the Boundaries Commissioners to remove other parts of Latrobe City Council, with around 4,000 voters in Boolarra, Cowwarr, Toongabbie and Yinnar transferred to Gippsland South.

In two-party preferred terms, Moe recorded a Labor majority of 2.4% in 2018, and the transfer of Labor voting Moe into Morwell increases the notional Labor majority to an estimated 4.0%. With Russell Northe retiring at the 2022 election, a two-candidate preferred margin versus Labor has not been calculated and Morwell is now listed as a notional Labor seat.

Background

Morwell was created in 1955 and held by the Liberal Party until 1970. Morwell was first won for Labor by Derek Amos in 1970, and he was succeeded at a 1981 by-election by Valerie Callister. At the 1982 Victorian Election, Labor's Barry Murphy surprisingly won Gippsland Province in the Legislative Council, something that caused problems for Labor when Callister retired as member for Morwell in 1988. With little chance of re-election to the Legislative Council, Murphy wanted to contest Morwell, and eventually ran as an Independent after Labor chose Keith Hamilton as its candidate. Murphy's challenge weakened Labor's margin in Morwell at the 1988 election, which meant Morwell was one of the only seats to swing to Labor on the defeat of the Kerner government in 1992.

Hamilton retired at the 2002 election. Labor's majority was halved by the presence of an anti-government independent campaigning with the support of the CFMEU over the government's decision to close old-growth forests to loggers. In 2006, a Labor proposal to tap local water supplies in return for re-cycled Melbourne waste water to cool power plants raised voter ire and led to Labor's Brendan Jenkins being defeated, though the surprise was that Russell Northe won the seat for the Nationals. Northe was re-elected with a 14.1% boost in his margin at the 2010 election, the largest swing against Labor in the state.

The 2014 election saw Morwell return to being a marginal seat, Northe re-elected but after an 11.5% swing to Labor. It was the second election in a row where Morwell recorded the largest swing in the state.

Northe's resignation as a National MP and nomination in 2018 as an Independent created a complex contest. In a field of 11 candidates, Labor led on first preferences with 34.2% to Northe in second on 19.6%. Liberal and National preferences played a crucial role in Northe winning re-election.

Polling Place Results 2018

The map of polling places below includes the old and new boundaries for Morwell and shows why the seat is strengthened for Labor with the addition of Moe. All three polling places in Moe transferred from Narracan recorded Labor two-party results above 63%.

Of the 20 polling places in the new Morwell used at the 2018 election, 14 recorded Labor two-party preferred majorites. Labor two party results ranged from 36.0% at Glengarry Primary School to 70.1% at Baringa Special School in Moe.

Past Voting Patterns

No electorate in Victoria has been forced to undergo as much social change as Morwell over the last three decades. In the 1990s there was privatisation of the electricity industry. In 2006 there were proposals for Melbourne to tap into the local water supply in exchange for treated water to cool the power plants. Now there are power plant closures to achieve the nation's climate change targets.

With that background is it any wonder that Morwell has drifted across the political spectrum, Labor leaning up to 1999, more Coalition leaning since.

More Information

Dale Harriman

Liberal

Harriman has lived in the Latrobe Valley for 30 years and has worked for and run small and medium sized businesses, mostly in the hospitality industry. He is serving a fourth term as an East Ward Councillor on Latrobe City Council and is currently employed as a Business Development Manager at an engineering company. In the past he has been Australian President of the National Timber Councils Association and has been involved with a number of local welfare and sporting organisations.

Website

Martin Cameron

The Nationals

Cameron describes himself as a born and bred Traralgon boy. He is a plumber byy trade, runs his own small business, and has had a lifelong involvement in the Traralgon Football Netball Club. He has been a plumber for 35 years, for the past 20 running the business that his dad and mum set up.

Rochelle Hine

Australian Greens

Hine has spent most of her life living in rural Victoria and moved to GunaiKurnai Country (Gippsland) in 2020. She has a background and many decades working in health and is currently employed as a lecturer in rural health. She specialises in mental health and leads projects aimed at fostering social and systems change to create healthy and equitable environments. Hine has also held various voluntary positions; as a board member of a family violence service, a school council president, leader of community groups and has been secretary of the Gippsland Greens Branch for the past year.

Jessica McAuliffe

Animal Justice Party

McAuliffe states that she has trumpeted the plight of animals from a young age, which was not an easy task growing up in prime dairy farming country in South Gippsland. She now lives in Morwell with her husband, 15-year-old staffy and 2 rescue cats. McAuliffe is currently studying her Juris Doctor (law) at Melbourne RMIT and assisting at a Gippsland family law firm.

Kate Maxfield

Australian Labor Party - Victorian Branch

Maxfield first ran for parliament when she was a medical student at Monash University, contesting Gippsland East unsuccessfully in 2014. She is now qualified and before the election was working as an emergency department Doctor at Latrobe Regional Hospital. She describes herself as a seventh-generation Gippslander and a Morwell local. She is the daughter of Ian Maxfield who was the Labor member for neighbouring Narracan from 1999 to 2006.
